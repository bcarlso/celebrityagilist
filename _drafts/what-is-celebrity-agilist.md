Celebrity Agilist started as a joke towards Brandon Carlson, the IT Nerd. Brandon has
been apart of the agile community for more than ten years. He loves
telling stories from his experience that all have an embedded lession.
He doesn't push the lesson, but he allows the people he's talking to
discover it. His style of highly effective for people to learn and yet
he gives the impression that he doesn't even know he's doing it.

He also describes himself as the 'IT Nerd'. Not as a 'Thought Leader' or
consultant or coach. He doesn't self promote, but he does get
recognized for his contributions. As a joke, I made him a T-Shirt for this
years Agile Alliance conference in Nashville, Tennessee.

Brandon is anti-thesis of a 'Celebrity'. He isn't flashy. His preferred waredrobe
is a collection of software conference and vendor t-shirts. He doesn't
promote himself, but after every conversation his audience grows.

When I shared the photo of Brandon wearing the T-Shirt, I had people
asking for their own shirt. To continue the joke, I registered the
celebrityagilist.com domain and quickly forgot about it after the
conference was over.

After a couple weeks I started to think about all the people that are
interested in improving their work, their teams and themselves. They
don't want to be 

Ten years ago the Agile community was working hard to have managers
listen to us about an alternatve way of getting work done. The Agile
mindset was harder to get explain than the prescritive processes of
Extreme Programming and Scrum. The leaders of the community became know
to the community. I'm not going to fall into the trap of naming any of
them here, since the people I name may not appriceate it, but more
importantly, the people I don't name will be offended.

That gets me to the point of Celebrity Agilist. You don't have to be
famous to make an impact on the community, your organization or your
team. Do good work, share your knowledge and help others.
